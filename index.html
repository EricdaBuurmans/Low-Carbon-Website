Low Carbon Web Design

Looking into the ways we can
reduce our carbon footprint online

Low carbon web design focuses on reducing the carbon footprint of websites by optimizing code, images, and servers to consume less energy, contributing to a more sustainable digital ecosystem. Its importance lies in mitigating the environmental impact of the internet, as energy-efficient websites help decrease greenhouse gas emissions and promote a greener online presence.

How We Can Fix It

There are many different techniques we can use to reduce strain on the data centers running our websites. The following are some of those techniques:

  • Optimise the code by writing efficient code.
  • Compress and dither images.
  • Reduce site animations
  • Use default typefaces.
  • No third-party tracking/advirtising/cookies.
  • Streamline the interactions in your site.

A Few Examples of Low Carbon Design

Below are some visual examples of how I've reduced this sites carbon footprint.

Used dithered images

Dithered images simulate colours that are not present in the actual image's colour palette. Arranging pixels and noise in different ways can help to simulate the full range of colour.

Used a default typeface

I've converted all text in this site to Times New Roman. This is a typeface which is already pre-installed on all computers, so it doesnt use any more data to download and display them.

Reduced animations & removed data heavy content

In the original template, the side navigation has animations - same with some hover elements. I've also removed the contact section as that displayed more intensive elements. I also replaced the social media icons with simple text links.

Why low carbon web design may be important to the design profession in the future

Low carbon web design will only continue to become more relevant as our environmental awareness increases. There is a rising demand for these eco-friendly practices across different industries, design is no different. Low carbon web design also reduces strain on data centers and therefore can make these systems more efficient and save corporations lots of money - there may be incentive to reduce each sites load on these centers. Companies may also opt for more eco-friendly designs as they could see it as more future-proof, particularly if there is incentive from government regulation in the future.
